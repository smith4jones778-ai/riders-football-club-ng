Riders FC Board Meeting Minutes

Month: December 2025
Time: 10:30 AM

Attendance

All board members were present at the meeting. Attendance included the Chairman, Vice Chairman, Secretary, Treasurer, Directors, and the two club coaches.

Agenda

  • Opening Prayer
  • Chairman’s Address
  • Reading and Adoption of Previous Minutes
  • Matters Arising
  • Non-Participation in the 2025 Dare to Dream Competition
  • Coaches’ Remarks
  • Any Other Business
  • Closing

Discussion

The meeting commenced at about 10:30 AM with an opening prayer led by Baba T.

The Chairman thereafter addressed members, encouraging everyone to remain committed to the club’s objectives and growth. He expressed concern over the noticeable decline in members’ rate of contribution and urged improved commitment.

The Secretary read the minutes of the previous meeting. No corrections or omissions were raised. The adoption of the minutes was moved by Mal. Hassan and seconded by Akeem, after which the minutes were unanimously adopted.

Under matters arising, the issue of the club’s non-participation in the 2025 Dare to Dream Competition was extensively discussed. Members generally agreed that the primary reason was the shortage of players who were available and prepared to travel for the competition.

The coaches addressed the board and advised that the club should restrategize its operations, particularly in player development, recruitment, and preparedness, to avoid similar challenges in future competitions.

Resolutions / Decisions

  • The minutes of the previous meeting were adopted without amendment.
  • The board agreed that inadequate player availability was the main factor behind the club’s absence from the 2025 Dare to Dream Competition.
  • The board accepted the coaches’ recommendation to restrategize club operations ahead of future competitions.

Action Items

  • The Secretary is to circulate the adopted minutes to all members.
  • The Coaches, in collaboration with the board, are to develop a revised operational and player-development strategy.
  • Members are encouraged to improve regularity and commitment to financial contributions.
